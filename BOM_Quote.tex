  At 7.5\,A through AWG12 over 5\,m: round-trip drop~=~$7.5\,\mathrm{A} \times 52\,\mathrm{m\Omega} = \mathbf{0.39\,V}$.

\section{4.\enspace Engineering Notes \& Recommendations}
% ============================================================

\begin{tabular}{L{3.5cm} L{\dimexpr\linewidth-3.5cm-4\tabcolsep\relax}}
  \HDR{Topic} & \HDR{Detail} \\

  \rowcolor{rowB}
  \makecell[tl]{%
    \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}Level Shifter\\
    \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}(REQUIRED)}
  &
  \small ESP32 GPIO high~=~3.3\,V;
  WS2815 $V_\mathrm{ih,min}$~=~3.5\,V.
  Margin:~\textbf{$-$200\,mV} (violation).
  The SN74AHCT125N accepts 3.3\,V input
  ($V_\mathrm{ih,min}$~=~2.0\,V) and drives output to full 5\,V.
  Omitting this causes intermittent ``zombie pixel'' failures that vary
  by LED batch, temperature, and cable length. \\

  \makecell[tl]{%
    \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}Power Injection\\
    \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}(RECOMMENDED)}
  &
  \sloppy\small External wire drop (AWG12, 5\,m, 7.5\,A)~=~\textbf{0.39\,V}.
  Strip entry sees \textbf{11.61\,V} --- above WS2815 minimum of \textbf{11.5\,V}.
  However, internal strip trace resistance ($\approx$30\,m$\Omega$/m) adds further
  drop at high brightness. Inject 12\,V/GND at \textbf{both ends} of each strip. \\

  \rowcolor{rowB}
  \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}Wire Gauge
  &
  \small Use \textbf{AWG12} for the 12\,V supply bus and strip runs
  (7.5\,A at 12\,V; 0.39\,V drop over 5\,m --- within WS2815 spec).
  Use AWG22 for DIN data lines. \\

  \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}Buck Derating
  &
  \small WS2815 strips connect \textbf{directly at 12\,V} --- no strip buck needed.
  ESP32: one XL4015 5\,A per unit, adequate for 0.5\,A max draw.
  Mount heatsinks; derate to 70\% for continuous operation. \\

  \rowcolor{rowB}
  \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}WLED Setup
  &
  \small Navigate to \texttt{Config\,$\to$\,LED Preferences}:
  set \textbf{LED Type = WS2815} (not WS2812B) and
  \textbf{Maximum Current = 6000\,mA} per strip
  (protects a 10\,A PSU; raise to 7500\,mA if using LRS-150-12).
  Synchronise both ESP32 units via WLED multicast UDP
  (port~21324, same subnet). \\

  \small\bfseries\color{bluefg}Fusing
  &
  \small Install a \textbf{10\,A blade fuse} on the 12\,V feed of each system.
  Strip worst-case current is 7.5\,A; 10\,A fuse provides protection with margin.
  Do not use 25\,A fuses --- they will not protect AWG12 wiring at strip-level faults. \\

  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!4!white, colframe=blue!40!black,
  title={\bfseries PSU Sizing Note --- 10\,A vs 12.5\,A},
  fontupper=\small\RaggedRight]

  \textbf{Q: Is a 10\,A / 220\,V\,$\to$\,12\,V PSU sufficient?}

  \textbf{Yes}, for typical use.
  The 7.5\,A figure is the absolute worst case: all 300 LEDs at
  \emph{full white} (R\,+\,G\,+\,B at 100\%) simultaneously.
  In practice, colorful effects draw \textbf{2--4\,A} per strip.

  \vspace{4pt}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}l l l@{}}
    \textbf{Scenario} & \textbf{Strip current} & \textbf{10\,A PSU load} \\
    \midrule
    Full white, 100\% (worst case) & 7.5\,A  & 75\% \\
    Colorful effects, 80\%         & $\approx$3--5\,A & 30--50\% \\
    Single colour, 50\%            & $\approx$1.5\,A  & 15\% \\
  \end{tabular}

  \vspace{4pt}
  A 10\,A PSU is acceptable provided:
  (1) WLED maximum current is set to \textbf{7500\,mA};
  (2) the PSU is a quality unit (Mean Well or equivalent), not a no-brand transformer.

  The LRS-150-12 (12.5\,A) in the BOM provides a better safety margin and is recommended.
  Both systems are independent --- simultaneous use has no effect on per-system sizing.
\end{tcolorbox}
